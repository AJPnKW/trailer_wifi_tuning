Safe design position

Design ruleReason
Default all utility actions to read-only.Prevents configuration drift and accidental link loss during discovery.
Separate discovery from change execution.Allows safer architecture and clearer validation boundaries.
Detect capabilities from the device and firmware, not from assumption.Documentation has changed over time and may vary by region/version.
Do not design around root shell access.No current evidence justifies that assumption.
Keep the workbench aligned to the broader trailer Wi-Fi system.The CPE is one interdependent subsystem, not a disconnected standalone product effort.

Target utility architecture

LayerPurposeNotes
Discovery layerIdentify reachable device, firmware family, management ports, and export file characteristics.Read-only.
Adapter layerHandle web-derived workflows, export parsing, optional SSH probing, and optional SNMP probing.Capability gated.
Audit layerConvert raw findings into risk, compatibility, and configuration guidance.Structured findings with severity.
GUI layerProvide multi-tab guided views, defaults, field help, warnings, and report/export actions.Python-first; avoid Tkinter.
Report layerPersist findings and status back into repo-compatible artifacts.Supports drift control and future thread continuity.

Planned GUI characteristics

  • Tabbed layout rather than a single long form.
  • Multi-column field grouping.
  • Combination of checkboxes, radio buttons, dropdowns, text inputs, and larger explanatory text panels.
  • Built-in coaching notes for purpose, impact, suggested defaults, and safe ranges.
  • Clear distinction between observed values, suggested values, and user-entered override values.
  • Report/export actions to persist findings without requiring fragile manual note-taking.
